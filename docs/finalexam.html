c)

In assessing effect of private schools Muralidharan and Sundaram (2015) stressthe importance of accounting for two kinds of spillovers. Elaborate (2 points)

A group of students from a public school is moved to a private school in exchange of a bonus. Then there exist two spillover effects, one is on the private school students that receive to their new classmates (the ones that came from the public school) and the other is on the students that remain on the public school.

If the public school students that moved to the private school have poorer skills than required, they will bias the overall performance downwards.

If the public school students that moved to the private school have better skills and attitude than rest of the students, those students left behind in their public school will have an even worse performance, due to the lack of interaction with better skilled pairs.

It is necessary to account for all the possible spillover effects in order to avoid biased conclusions.
